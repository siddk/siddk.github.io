I am a PhD Student in computer science at Stanford University where I'm grateful to be co-advised by Percy Liang and Dorsa Sadigh.
I am honored to be supported by the Open Philanthropy Project AI Fellowship.
    Research: I focus on human-robot interaction and natural language processing. My research focuses on two axes:
    • Large-scale pretraining and representation learning (developing better "raw materials" for robotics & language)
    • Better language & shared autonomy for robotics (interactive, human applications) by employing these materials!
    Timeline: I recently completed a year-long part-time research internship at Hugging Face ü§ó working on multimodal pretraining and scaling with an incredible team of collaborators.
      Before Stanford, I was a resident at Facebook AI Research in New York, where I was lucky to work with Rob Fergus, Douwe Kiela, Jason Weston, and Arthur Szlam on grounded language understanding. Here's a short Q&A I did about my residency.
        Prior to the residency, I was lucky to do two summer internships during college at Bloomberg Research with wonderful mentors Gideon Mann and David Rosenberg, where we worked on applying machine learning to software engineering.
          I was an undergraduate at Brown University, where I concentrated in computer science and literary arts. While at Brown, I did research in NLP & human-robot interaction advised by Stefanie Tellex and Eugene Charniak.
